<h1><u>MRI Books</u></h1>

<column1><img src="http://static.lulu.com/browse/product_thumbnail.php?productId=22860713&resolution=320"></column1>
<column2><a href="http://www.lulu.com/shop/dwight-nishimura/principles-of-magnetic-resonance-imaging/hardcover/product-22860713.html">Dwight Nishimura. Principles of Magnetic Resonance Imaging</a>. <br>
Nishimura's text is an undergraduate-level MRI text which provides a solid mathematics and physics foundation for NRM/MRI, and goes into an appropriate amount of detail in each topic for a broad understanding of NMR/MRI. This is the textbook used in the undergraduate offering of the <a href="NMR.html">NMR/MRI course</a>.<br></column2>
<br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br>

<column1><img src="thumbnails/callaghan.png"></column1>
<column2>
<a href="https://www.amazon.com/Principles-Nuclear-Magnetic-Resonance-Microscopy/dp/0198539975/ref=sr_1_2?ie=UTF8&qid=1533833184&sr=8-2&keywords=callaghan+mri">Paul Callaghan, Principles of Nuclear Magnetic Resonance Microscopy.</a> <br>
Callaghan's text is approachable from nearly all scientific backgrounds and is the course textbook for Dr Thomen's <a href="NMR.html">NMR/MRI course</a>. It contains thorough descriptions of NMR physics mostly focusing on the semi-classical description. It has only cursory descriptions of the quantum picture of NMR, MRI physics, and k-Space.<br></column2>
<br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br>

<column1><img src="https://media.wiley.com/product_data/coverImage300/52/04717208/0471720852.jpg"></column1>
<column2><a href = "https://www.amazon.com/Magnetic-Resonance-Imaging-Physical-Principles/dp/0471720852/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1533833320&sr=8-1&keywords=haacke+mri&dpID=41RT2InwHEL&preST=_SX218_BO1,204,203,200_QL40_&dpSrc=srch">Robert Brown, et al. Magnetic Resonance Imaging: Physical Principles and Sequence Design 2nd Edition</a>  <br>Brown's MRI text in considered a staple of MRI laboratories and graduate courses in MRI. This text is the de facto reference text on MRI - and I do mean to emphasize that it is a <i>reference</i> text. While it is thorough, the student approaching NMR/MRI physics for the first time may find it overbearing..<br></column2>
<br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br>

<column1><img src="https://images-na.ssl-images-amazon.com/images/I/41znnTKxc0L._SX326_BO1,204,203,200_.jpg"></column1>
<column2><a href = "https://www.amazon.com/Handbook-Pulse-Sequences-Matt-Bernstein/dp/0120928612/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1533834124&sr=8-1&keywords=mri+pulse+sequences">Matt Bernstein, et al. Handbook of MRI Pulse Sequences</a>  <br> Although it's called a "handbook" making it seem like a mere reference text, one can learn a great deal about various MRI pulse sequences, rf and gradient shapes, and even basic Fourier Theory from this text. When interested in a particular branch of MRI pulse sequences, especially when reading a journal article which references the numerous acronyms which are thrown around in MR circles, this book should be the first stop.<br></column2>
<br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br><br>

<column1><img src="https://images-na.ssl-images-amazon.com/images/I/41Vi4lSu62L._SX324_BO1,204,203,200_.jpg"></column1>
<column2><a href = "https://www.amazon.com/Principles-Magnetic-Resonance-Springer-Solid-State/dp/3540501576">Charles Slichter, Principles of Magnetic Resonance.</a>  <br> As a physicist, this is my favorite text on NMR/MRI. This is because, the text is very readable and intuative for those who have a strong background in . The non-physicists may find this book difficult to follow, but I have yet to find a better and more succinct explanation of NMR quantum physics.<br></column2>
